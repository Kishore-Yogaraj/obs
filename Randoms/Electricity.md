### Voltage
Voltage is how much electricity is being sent through the circuit

### Amps
Amps is how fast the electricity is being sent through the circuit

### Resistance
Resistance determines how difficult it is for current to flow through a circuit

### Watt
Watts are how much electrical energy (can be any energy) is used per second
- It is the unit used to measure power

### Powering a Bulb
Let's say we want to power a bulb

First thing we need to take not of his how many volts the bulb is rated for. This will tell us the voltage of the power supply we end up using. Let's say its 12 volts for now.

We connect the bulb to the power source using a wire. The bulb will also have an internal resistance which determines how much current it receives. Too much current means the bulb will over heat, too little current means the bulb will not shine as bright as it can.

Based on this internal resistance, it will determine how much current can flow through the wires. Let's say the internal resistance of the bulb is around 6 ohms, using I = V/R we know that the bulb can safely operate at 6 amps.

This means that we have to make sure the wire itself is rated for 6 amps so that it can carry the current required by the bulb without overheating

$$
P = V * I
$$
$$
P = V^2/R
$$
$$
I = V/R
$$
