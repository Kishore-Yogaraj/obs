Now that we know what topics can be covered in interviews, we have to go and study how to use them. When I'm studying for a test, I usually follow a tutorial on how to answer a question, attempt a practice question, take note of where I get stuck, figure out why I got stuck and what I needed to understand so I don't get stuck again, take careful notes of how to avoid the part of the solution where you got stuck, and then move on to the next question and rinse and repeat.

Here's the breakdown of our studying approach:
- Follow a tutorial on how to answer that specific type of question
- Attempt a practice question you haven't seen before
- Note at what point you get stuck
- Figure out why you got stuck and what you need to do differently
- Take detailed notes on how to avoid getting stuck next time
- Move on to the next question and rinse and repeat

**During this whole process you should take note of how to recognize different patterns as well which is provided in the resource shared above and below. Being able to recognize it is how you will do well on interviews.**
