
## Perception Team Member

### Essential Skills Required

- **ROS2 fundamentals**: Understanding of nodes, topics, publishers/subscribers, and message types
- **Hardware interfacing**: Experience with camera drivers, UART/USB communication protocols
- **Basic computer vision**: Understanding of image processing pipelines and object detection concepts
- **Linux command line proficiency**: Ability to navigate filesystems, manage processes, and debug hardware connections
- **Programming skills**: Python or C++ for ROS2 node development
- **Documentation writing**: Ability to create clear, step-by-step technical documentation

### Skills That Can Be Learned On The Job

- **Advanced computer vision**: YOLO, SSD, or other object detection architectures
- **Sensor fusion techniques**: Combining data from multiple sensor modalities
- **ROS2 advanced topics**: Custom message types, service calls, parameter management
- **Hardware troubleshooting**: Debugging sensor communication issues and driver problems
- **Performance optimization**: Real-time processing and computational efficiency
- **Calibration techniques**: Camera and lidar calibration procedures

### Ideal Background

Someone with robotics coursework, embedded systems experience, or computer vision projects. A hands-on learner who enjoys working with hardware and isn't afraid to debug driver issues. Previous experience with OpenCV, sensor integration, or real-time systems is valuable.

## Localization Team Member

### Essential Skills Required

- **Mathematical foundation**: Strong understanding of probability, statistics, and linear algebra
- **State estimation theory**: Knowledge of Kalman filters, particularly Extended Kalman Filters (EKF)
- **Coordinate systems**: Understanding of GPS coordinates, transformations, and reference frames
- **ROS2 proficiency**: Experience with tf2 transforms, robot_localization package
- **Sensor data interpretation**: Understanding IMU, GPS, and odometry data characteristics
- **Simulation experience**: Familiarity with CARLA or similar robotics simulators

### Skills That Can Be Learned On The Job

- **Advanced filtering techniques**: Unscented Kalman Filters, Particle Filters
- **SLAM algorithms**: Simultaneous Localization and Mapping techniques
- **Error analysis**: Understanding and quantifying localization accuracy and drift
- **Multi-sensor fusion**: Advanced techniques for combining heterogeneous sensor data
- **Real-world testing**: Designing and executing systematic validation experiments
- **Optimization techniques**: Tuning filter parameters for best performance

### Ideal Background

Engineering or computer science student with strong math skills and control systems or robotics experience. Someone who enjoys systematic testing and validation. Experience with MATLAB/Simulink, control theory, or navigation systems is highly valuable.

## Mapping and Planning Team Member

### Essential Skills Required

- **Graph theory and algorithms**: Understanding of shortest path algorithms, graph representation
- **Geospatial data**: Experience with GPS coordinates, mapping formats (OSM), and geographic information systems
- **Software integration**: Ability to work with external libraries and APIs (LaneLet2)
- **Path planning fundamentals**: Understanding of waypoint generation and route optimization
- **File format parsing**: Working with XML, JSON, or other structured data formats
- **Programming skills**: Strong Python or C++ for algorithm implementation

### Skills That Can Be Learned On The Job

- **Advanced path planning**: A*, RRT, or other sophisticated planning algorithms
- **Map processing**: Creating, editing, and optimizing map representations
- **Route optimization**: Multi-objective optimization for path planning
- **Real-time planning**: Dynamic replanning and obstacle avoidance
- **Mapping tools**: Professional GIS software and mapping frameworks
- **Graph optimization**: Techniques for efficient graph processing and storage

### Ideal Background

Computer science student with algorithms coursework or someone with GIS/mapping experience. Enjoys working with data structures and spatial problems. Previous experience with navigation systems, game development (pathfinding), or geographic information systems is ideal.

## Action/Control Team Member

### Essential Skills Required

- **Control systems theory**: Understanding of PID controllers, feedback systems, and control loops
- **Vehicle dynamics**: Basic understanding of car kinematics and steering mechanics
- **Real-time programming**: Experience with time-critical systems and control loops
- **ROS2 control**: Understanding of velocity and steering commands in robotics
- **Mathematical modeling**: Ability to implement and tune mathematical control algorithms
- **Safety mindset**: Understanding of fail-safes and safety-critical system design

### Skills That Can Be Learned On The Job

- **Advanced control algorithms**: Model Predictive Control (MPC), adaptive control
- **Vehicle-specific tuning**: Understanding car-specific dynamics and limitations
- **Path following techniques**: Advanced pure pursuit variations, Stanley controller
- **System integration**: Connecting control systems with perception and planning modules
- **Performance optimization**: Achieving smooth, stable vehicle control
- **Testing methodologies**: Systematic validation of control performance

### Ideal Background

Mechanical, electrical, or aerospace engineering student with controls coursework. Someone who enjoys hands-on testing and iterative improvement. Previous experience with control systems, robotics, or RC vehicles is valuable. Must be comfortable with both simulation and real-world testing.

## Cross-Team Skills Valuable for All Members

### Technical Skills

- **Git version control**: Essential for collaborative development
- **Debugging skills**: Systematic approach to identifying and fixing issues
- **System integration**: Understanding how different modules interact
- **Testing and validation**: Designing experiments to verify system performance

### Soft Skills

- **Communication**: Ability to document work and explain technical concepts
- **Collaboration**: Working effectively in a multidisciplinary team
- **Problem-solving**: Breaking down complex problems into manageable tasks
- **Adaptability**: Willingness to learn new tools and technologies as needed

### Recommended Team Composition

- **2-3 people on Perception** (highest priority, most hardware-intensive)
- **1-2 people on Localization** (mathematically intensive)
- **1 person on Mapping/Planning** (can be combined with another role)
- **1-2 people on Action/Control** (requires extensive testing and tuning)

The most critical combination is having strong perception and localization teams, as these provide the foundation for everything else to work properly.
