 **"It's far better to hack and slash your way through life with a dull sword than to spend your entire life with a sharp one"**

Self improvement books only work if you actually apply what you read. The average person who picks up 1 self improvement book purchase 7 more self improvement books. These books give you the impression that you're learning and making progress without actually learning or making any progress.

What's the point in learning anything if you don't actually apply it to your life. You're creating this fake image of progress and productivity when in reality its just another book that went in through one year and out the other. There's some kind of superior feeling about reading these books that makes it addicting but it's not that great unless you put it into practice. 

With that said, these books help you develop new ideas about your current way of life. Its important to actually go out and experience the things you want to learn about and then supplement that with other information you learn. It's still good to read books. It's great for the brain, a good conversation starter, improves your vocabulary, and a much better alternative to doom scrolling all the time. 

In Atomic Habits, although I won't apply a majority of the information taught in the book, there were a few key ideas that really stood out to me that I see being applicable to my life which is why I found use in the book and enjoyed reading it. 

The reason I read these self improvement books are not because I believe these books will help create this perfect life I dream of. That reading these books will get me jacked or super wealthy or bring me eternal happiness and a life of no struggle. I read these books for one, I need to pick up something better to do in my time than doom scroll and two, these books still deliver a couple messages that stick and can be incorporated into my life. These books will never and should never act as a way of life to a person. If they choose to do so then be it, that's okay too. But for me, the main purpose of reading these books is to just see what ideas people have to share about a certain area of life that I'm interested and pick up on a few points I think are useful and can be incorporated into the life I'm living right now. **The best teacher is experience.** 

I actually saw this while reading Atomic Habits. Some of the topics and pieces of advice that was provided were things I already learned through just going through life. An example of this was to start using a habit tracker. I started using one before I read this book because I got joy out of keeping a streak of good habits. If anything, this book reinforced the idea of using a habit tracker. This is an example of how I used a book to reinforce my experiences through life and see that other people also discover similar tools to help them in a certain area of life.

Highly successful people didn't get there by reading a bunch of self improvement books. They got there by being incredibly good at something and working hard at it. Elon Musk and Bill Gates didn't read atomic habits and then become successful.

Every message shared in a book is not novel. It comes from the past. Comes from religion, philosophers, scientists, etc. but it's just delivered in a different package. A package you are more accepting of as human.

An example of this is in Man's Search for Meaning by Victor E Frankl. This entire book can be boiled down to a quote by Prussian Philosopher: Friedrich Nietzsche: "A man who has a why can bear any how ". Nietzsche's quote can be boiled down to Aristotle's belief that happiness can only be achieved when we're serving some higher purpose.

So read these books not as a means for the book to give you a way to live life but read these books to reinforce the ideas you already have about your life and what new ideas you can add on to it.

### How to read books and retain information

The best way to read these informational books is to take note of each chapter. Doesn't have to be anything in depth. Just a couple notes about some key points you think that stood out to you and a brief summary about what the chapter was about. Anything that really aligns with your life will so clearly stand out to you and it'll be easy to incorporate it. A lot of the things these books say are already pretty obvious they just do a good job reinforcing it and sometimes ways you can actually reinforce it. When you're out and about and reading an informational book, just jot down some important points or points that stood out to you and a brief summary about what the chapter was about. Don't write notes for the sake of writing notes. Only write notes that clearly stand out to you. These are the points that will actually stick with you. You want purpose out of these books. Not an imagination of productivity and progress.
