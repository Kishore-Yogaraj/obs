#### Summary
This was an introductory chapter to the book that just talks about what the book is about and how it will attempt to look at things a lot deeper than just at the surface level. A really cool piece in this chapter was how Roe V Wade actually reduced the crime rate from the 1990s to 2005. Be careful when reading this book because you don't want to treat it like a bible. Meaning that the hidden meanings that this book uncovers doesn't actually mean that its right. However, from this book we should take away how there's sometimes a deeper side to simple questions if you know how to look properly.

#### Key points
- Crime rate was incredibly high in the 1990s
- People thought it would rise by 100% in 1995 but instead it fell by more than 50%
- People thought the reason for this was because of the roaring 1990s economy which helped turn back crime
- People thought because gun control laws were implemented crime dropped
- People though it was because of the innovative policing strategies put into place by New York City
- The reason why the author believes this isn't true is because this would mean the power to stop criminals had been within reach this whole time and it should've been done earlier
- Apparently the story of Norma McCorvey was the reason for the drop in crime rate
- She was a poor woman and got pregnant in Texas where abortion was illegal.
- More powerful people heard of her case and she became the lead plaintiff
- Her name was disguised as Jane Row
- In 1973 the court ruled in favor of Jane Row and legalized abortion 
- It was found that most of the woman who needed abortions were those that were poor and in bad neighborhoods and when they had kids, those kids were more likely to fall into the life of crime
- Since abortion was legalized, a lot less kids were being born into unconventional lives which means by the time 2005 rolled around where these kids would have been involved in crime at the time didn't end up existing
- Roe V Wade was the reason why crime had significantly reduced from 1990 to 2005
- Many people argue against this as it speaks on correlation rather than causation
- One of the main reasons this can't be true is that crime rate actually dropped in the older crowd before the younger crowd
- It also ignores differences among individual women within a crime prone population
	- Women who were more likely to get abortions are also more forward thinking meaning that their kids would have been less likely to commit crimes
- Main reasons for the drop in crime
	- End of crack cocaine in the 1980
	- Increase police and incarceration (prison population increased)
	- Decreased lead exposure
		- Children born with lower lead exposure in the late 1970s and 1980 reached their peak crime-committing years in the early 1990s just as crime rates began to decline
		- Lead is a neurotoxin that can cause cognitive and behavioral problems including violence
