### What is a bandsaw
- A bandsaw is used to make cuts through aluminum between angles of 45 to 90 degrees
- **Not sure if you can cut steel with a bandsaw**
- There's two types of bandsaws you can use to make your cuts
	- There's a smaller bandsaw used for smaller material which can found on the table next to the sanding machine
	- The bigger one can be found near the machine shop office

### How to use a bandsaw
- **Set the angle**
	- There's two gray plates on the bandsaw which the bandsaw comes between 
	- On the right plate there is a black nob on top of the plate which can be loosened and tighten to change the angle
	- If you want to to change the angle first loosen this black knob and then set the band saw in its down position
	- After this use the actual handle of the bandsaw to rotate to your desired angle
	- After setting angle lock the bandsaw in place using the black knob on top of the right hand side plate
- **Use the wheel at the front of the bandsaw to open the clamps to place your piece inside**
	- Turn counter clockwise to place the material inside and turn clockwise to lock piece in place
- **Align bandsaw with the cut line**
	- Move the bandsaw up and down and move the material left and right (loosen and tighten clamps to move material) until the bandsaw is align with your cut line
- **Start the bandsaw**
	- Bring the bandsaw all the way up and lock in place using the knob on the control panel (where the stop and start button are)
	- Turning the knob all the way clock wise locks the bandsaw in place when turned off and turning the knob all the way counter clockwise loosens the bandsaw and makes it easier top move and set manually when off
	- After the bandsaw is brought all the way up, hit start on the bandsaw and give it a couple seconds so that the coolant covers the entire blade
- **Make the cut**
	- Use the control knob to control the speed at which the bandsaw moves
	- Turning it all the way clockwise while on makes the bandsaw move slower and turning it all the way counter clockwise makes the bandsaw move faster
	- Adjust the speed as the bandsaw gets closer to the material
	- If you notice the bandsaw slowly down a lot when making the cut just increase the speed on it slowly until it makes a clean cut
	- The bandsaw will stop automatically after it makes a cut
- **Clean up**
	- Set the bandsaw back to the up position
	- Unclamp the material using the wheel 
	- Pull your material out and dry it off using the blue paper towels on your right
**Additional Notes**
- Same principles apply to the smaller bandsaw except you have to control the speed manually with your hand
- Ensure you bring the bandsaw down at a slow and steady pace and keep that pace until the cut is complete
