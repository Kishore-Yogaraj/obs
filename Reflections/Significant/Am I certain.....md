**Why I Am Not Optimizing for "Starting a Business From Scratch"**

Starting a business from zero is glorified because it makes for good stories, not because it is the highest-probability path to wealth.

The math is clear:
- Starting from scratch has a high failure rate
- Early years often produce little to no income
- Opportunity cost is enormous
	- Opportunity cost of any given action or decision is typically defined as the **value of the forgone alternative action or decision **
	- Opportunity cost is the loss of potential gain from other alternatives when on alternative is chosen
	- When choosing a business over becoming an operator, you realize that the opportunity cost is enormous because you could go years without making money and you miss out on a lot opportunities to build generational wealth later down the line
	- I want to pursue a role in engineering and become the best I can be and be strong in my role
- Risk is unbounded

Buying or partnering into existing cash-flowing businesses:
- Produces income immediatley
- Has structured downside
- Has a much higher success rates
- Allows leverage and compounding

My goal is not to prove anything to anyone.
I am trying to win this game **mathematically.**
