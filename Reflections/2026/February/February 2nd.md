### Little Snippet about Carbs
Our bodies break carbs into glucose. Glucose enters the bloodstream to be used as the brain's primary source of energy.

Simple carbs:
Carbs like rice cakes or bananas hit your blood stream fast. They burn hot and fast and cause a spike in energy. Good for right before you workout

Complex carbs:
Foods like oats or whole grain burn a lot slower

The regulator: **Insulin**
Our bodies naturally want our blood sugar to be at the perfect spot. Can't be too much or too little. When it spikes too high, the pancreas releases insulin

Insulin's job: It acts like a key that opens your cells to let the sugar in

If the spike was too fast, the body releases too much insulin. This clears the sugar our of your blood so quickly that your sugar level drop below where they started. This is the crash.

Our goal is to keep your blood sugar at a stable and flat level so the brain has a constant stream of energy and we never hit a shut down point.

The chaperone effect (proteins and fats):
Carbs alone will speed straight into the bloodstream which cause a spike and then a crash 

Proteins and fats will slow down the digestion of the carbs and instead of a spike you get a gentle hill.
