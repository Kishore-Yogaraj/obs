How is computer vision and machine learning used in home cameras and garage systems
Computer vision and machine learning are increasingly integrated into home camera and garage systems to enhance security, convenience, and automation. In home security cameras, computer vision algorithms are used to detect and recognize objects, people, and even specific activities. For example, machine learning models can differentiate between a person, a vehicle, or an animal, which helps reduce false alarms and allows the system to alert homeowners only when relevant events are detected.

One key application is in **motion detection** and **activity recognition**. Traditional systems detect any movement, but with machine learning, modern cameras can analyze the type of motion and identify whether it’s a person approaching the house or just a tree swaying in the wind. Advanced systems can even recognize familiar faces, using facial recognition models to allow known family members access while alerting homeowners to unknown visitors.

In garage systems, machine learning enhances **smart entry** and **security**. For instance, cameras combined with computer vision can automatically recognize a homeowner's car as it approaches and open the garage door without needing a remote. Similarly, these systems can detect if the garage door has been left open accidentally and send notifications or close the door autonomously.

Moreover, edge computing plays a significant role in these systems, allowing real-time analysis of video data directly on the device, reducing latency and ensuring privacy since data doesn’t need to be sent to the cloud for processing. These applications make home and garage systems more intelligent, secure, and responsive to user needs.
