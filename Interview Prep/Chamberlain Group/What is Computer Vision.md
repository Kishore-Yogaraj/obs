dComputer vision is a field of artificial intelligence that enables machines to interpret and understand visual information from the world, similar to how humans use their eyes and brain to make sense of what they see. It involves the processing and analysis of images or videos to extract meaningful data, which can then be used for tasks like object recognition, tracking, and scene understanding.

In practical terms, computer vision can be applied in a variety of areas, such as self-driving cars (to detect objects like pedestrians and lane lines), facial recognition systems, medical image analysis, and even augmented reality. Some common techniques used in computer vision include **edge detection**, **object detection** (like using YOLO or Faster R-CNN), and **image segmentation** to isolate important features in images.

At a high level, computer vision systems typically work by taking an input image, preprocessing it (for example, converting it to grayscale or reducing noise), and then using algorithms or machine learning models to identify patterns, features, or objects in the image. Once those patterns are identified, the system can make decisions based on that data, such as tracking objects or classifying what’s in the image.
