### Primary Focus Number 1: Playing the Button (BYN) and Cutoff (CO)

Play a very high percentage of hands when you are in the Button or Cutoff positions and play almost nothing from the early positions

**The Goal: Aggressive Stealing and Positional Advantage**
The primary goal is to aggressively open-raise whenever the action folds to you in these two positions

### Summary for Focus 1
There are three main positions to know which determine how you should play your cards:
- UTG/Middle
- Cut off
- Button

The further down the list you go the more hands you are able to play

Here are your top 10 cards:
AA, KK, QQ, JJ, TT, 99, AKs, AQs, AKo, KQs

Top 15 include the above and:
88, AJs, KJs, QJs, ATs

**Good idea to memorize the above**

The only cards you can play in the **UTG and Middle position** are your top 10 cards:
AA, KK, QQ, JJ, TT, 99, AKs, AQs, AKo, KQs

RAISE these cards to 3x to 4x the big blind

The cards you can play in the **Cut off position** are:
Top 15:
AA, KK, QQ, JJ, TT, 99, AKs, AQs, AKo, KQs
88, AJs, KJs, QJs, ATs

Good suited connectors:
T9s, 98s, 87s

Off suit Broadways (Goal here is to get a top pair with the draw)
AQo, KQo, AJo

The cards you can play in the **BTN Position** are:
Top 15:
AA, KK, QQ, JJ, TT, 99, AKs, AQs, AKo, KQs,
88, AJs, KJs, QJs, ATs

More suited connectors:
T9s, 98s, 87s
76s, 65s, 54s

Suited Gappers
KTs, QTs, J9s

Off suit Broadways
AQo, KQo, AJo
KJo, QJo
