**Driven Dimension:**
Reference dimension that is controlled by the model's geometry and are used for informational purposes. They are displayed as gray. When you change another dimension it will change this dimension as well
- Provide a reference or representation of the model's dimensions but does not change the geometry
- Modifying a driven dimension value will not affect the model it will only change the displayed reference value

**Driving Dimension:**
Directly controls and changes the geometry of the model.
- Dictate and define the size and shape of the model
- Changes to a driving dimension directly update associated geometry when the model is rebuilt
- Shown in black lines
- Used to define and constrain the model's features and dimensions

**Dimensioning (Over defined, under defined, fully defined)**
Dimensioning is used to define the size shape and position of a sketch or feature. 

A circle may have its radius but it has no relationships to anything in the area which means that it can still be moved around.

A rectangle may have its width and height but its bottom left corner may have no relationship to anything so it can be dragged around meaning its not fully defined

If it can be moved around, its not fully defined
